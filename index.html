Custom Cocktails

During my time as a bartender, I have been fortunate enough to have the opportunity to invent some of my own cocktails. The restaurant was a Japanese concept, so most of my personal recipies have an asian flair. Here is a short list.

The Ronin

The Ronin is a somewhat smokey whisky cocktail that I created as a twist on the classic Blood and Sand.

Ingredients

  • 1.5oz Japanese whisky
  • 0.5oz Umepon (Orange/Plum liquer)
  • 0.5oz Sweet Vermouth
  • 0.5oz Leopold Bros. Tart Cherry liquer

Directions

  1. Combine ingredients into a mixing glass.
  2. Add ice and stir for desired dilution.
  3. Serve up.
  4. Garnish with an expressed orange peel.

Tomo Collins

The "Tomo(Friend) Collins" is a cocktail named after a friend I met in Kyoto. It's similar to the classic Tom Collins, but with a fruity ginger twist.

Ingredients

  • 1.5oz Gin
  • 1oz Lemon juice
  • 0.5 oz Simple Syrup
  • 0.5oz Domain de Canton
  • 1 Strawberry

Directions

  1. Muddle the strawberry in a shaker tin.
  2. Add the other ingredients.
  3. Shake with ice to reach dersired dilution.
  4. Serve in a highball with a strawberry garnish.

Kuro Neko

This cocktail, although not asian influenced, is a play on a newly popular Jaguar cocktail. Hence the name "Black Cat".

Ingredients

  • 1.5oz Tequila Reposado
  • 0.75oz Amaro Montenegro
  • 0.75 Green Chartreuse

Directions

  1. Combine ingredients into a mixing glass.
  2. Stir to desired dilution.
  3. Serve on the rocks with a sprig of mint.
